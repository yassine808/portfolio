# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, engineering managers, and technical leads at software companies evaluating a 5th-year CS/Networks engineering student for a PFE internship. They scan fast. They've seen hundreds of portfolios. They care about: can this person build things, do they have taste, and are they serious.

Secondary audience: fellow developers and peers who might discover the site through GitHub or social links.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that communicates technical competence and design intentionality without spectacle. The goal is not to impress with animation — it's to make a recruiter stop scrolling and think "this person knows what they're doing." Every element should feel considered. Nothing decorative for decoration's sake.

Success: a visitor understands who Yassine is, what he's built, and how to contact him — in under 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Quiet. Opinionated.

Not loud, not playful, not "creative" in the decorative sense. Confident enough to leave things out. The voice of someone who reads Hacker News, uses a tiling window manager, and cares about typography but would never admit it.

## Anti-references

- No purple gradients, no neon glows, no glassmorphism
- No centered hero with a stock photo and a CTA button
- No "3-column feature cards" with icons above headings
- No Inter, Space Grotesk, Syne, Fraunces, or any reflex-reject font
- No emojis anywhere
- No startup-speak copy ("synergy," "next-gen," "seamless")
- No generic "John Doe" placeholder content
- No editorial-magazine aesthetic (no italic display serif, no drop caps, no broadsheet grid)
- No monospace-as-costume (this isn't a devtools product)

## Design Principles

1. **Earn every element.** If a section doesn't communicate something specific about skill, taste, or personality, it doesn't belong.
2. **Restraint is a power move.** White space isn't empty — it's confidence. Let the work breathe.
3. **Typography is the interface.** Choose fonts like you choose variable names: deliberately, with reason, never by reflex.
4. **Motion serves hierarchy.** Animate to guide attention, not to impress. If a reveal doesn't help someone understand what matters, cut it.
5. **Ship real content.** No lorem ipsum, no placeholder screenshots. Every project has a real description. Every skill is real.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — all motion is optional
- Sufficient color contrast on all text (4.5:1 minimum)
- Semantic HTML, proper heading hierarchy
- Keyboard-navigable
